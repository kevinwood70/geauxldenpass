Stretch Zone Golf Pilot

Could better mobility help lower a golfer’s handicap?

This pilot explores a simple but powerful idea: if a golfer moves better, feels better, and has fewer physical restrictions, could that translate into a more consistent swing and improved performance on the course?

While this study was not golf-specific, it provides a strong foundation to explore how improved mobility and reduced restriction may influence golf performance.

Why it matters for golf

Translating mobility into performance

  • More rotation – improved shoulder and hip mobility may allow for a fuller backswing and follow-through
  • Better sequencing – smoother coordination between hips, torso, and arms
  • Less compensation – fewer adjustments caused by tightness or restriction
  • Improved consistency – a body that moves more freely is easier to repeat
Key insight

Not a swing problem

Many golfers don’t have a swing problem — they have a mobility problem disguised as a swing problem.

The goal is not to rebuild a swing. It is to remove physical limitations that may be holding a golfer’s natural swing back.

Consistency

Cleaner movement patterns may lead to more consistent ball striking

Endurance

Less stiffness may help maintain performance throughout the round

Practice

Feeling better may lead to more frequent and higher-quality practice

Expected impact

What this could mean for a golfer

While results vary, improved mobility and reduced restriction often translate into meaningful performance gains over time.

  • High handicap golfers may see more noticeable improvements in movement and scoring
  • Mid handicap golfers may experience improved consistency and fewer bad swings
  • Low handicap golfers may benefit from marginal gains and injury prevention
Pilot

Jackson Champion – real-world case study

Jackson Champion will serve as the first participant in this golf performance pilot, helping us test how consistent stretching may influence mobility, comfort, and golf performance over time.

  • Establish a clear baseline
  • Follow a 30-day stretching protocol
  • Track mobility, comfort, and golf performance
  • Compare results and document findings
30-Day Plan

Simple and consistent approach

  • Baseline: assess mobility, comfort, and current golf performance
  • Stretch: 2–3 sessions per week
  • Track: note changes in body feel, swing feel, and performance
  • Review: compare before and after results
Integrity

What we can say with confidence

  • Practitioner-assisted stretching improves mobility and reduces restriction
  • For golfers, that may support a freer and more repeatable swing
  • This pilot is designed to test the real-world impact honestly
